Ammo

Types of Ammo

There were many types of ammunition, but only 4 are important.

The first one is AP shell. AP shell or Armor Piercing shell is designed to penetrate body armor or vehicle armor. AP projectiles were mainly used in warships, but after 1920s the shells were used for anti-tank missions. The concept of AP shell was so that the shell would leave the tank at very high muzzle velocity. However, as it slowed down the shell lost it's penetrating power.

The second one is APCR shell. APCR shell or Armor Piercing Composite Rigid shells are same as AP shells, but have much higher muzzle velocity. Their penetration power decreseas with range as well.

The thrid one is HEAT shell. HEAT or High Explosive Anti-Tank shells penetration power always stays the same. However, the HEAT shell is firend at very low muzzle velocity, and travels much slower than AP or APCR shell.

The forth one is HE shell. HE shell or High Explosive shell travels at almost same speed as AP shell. It's penetration power remains same no matter the distance. After the HE shell touches anything, it detonates and it's fragments can damage the vehicle nearby. They have the highest potentional damage but lowest penetration power.
